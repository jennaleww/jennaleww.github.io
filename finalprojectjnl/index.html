Young voters and their importance in the presidential election

By Jenna Lewis

Voting stickers-Unsplash

In the upcoming presidential election, Gen Z voters are actively encouraged to engage and participate in the process. Recognizing the pivotal role they play in shaping the future, organizations like the Center for Information & Research on Civic Living and Engagement (CIRCLE) are working to amplify the voices of young voters.

Their efforts are crucial, especially considering the significant disparity in youth turnout observed across different states. While some states have seen impressive participation rates as high as 37%, others have struggled with much turnout as low as 13%. These statistics show the importance of empowering and mobilizing Gen Z voters to ensure their voices are heard and their perspectives represented in the upcoming election.

In this survey taken by CIRCLE, data from 40 different states suggests that many of the states with high youth voter turnout in the 2022 midterm elections had strong policies that make it easier to register and vote. Turnout, in this case, is the percentage of eligible voters (whether or not they were registered) who cast a ballot. New York, Michigan, Pennsylvania, and Arkansas had the highest change in youth voters from the 2018 midterm election to the 2022 midterm election.

Medina believes that throughout the years and different elections, different issues sparked young voters' ideas on a candidate and the issues they support. “The gun violence prevention issue and the movement in 2018, after the Parkland shooting, was a galvanizing force for youth. In 2020, it was the racial justice movement after the George Floyd murder. In 2022, the abortion issue became a huge driver of youth engagement,” Medina says. “This year, the situation between Israel and Palestine has become important to many young people.”

Data Visualization

In November of 2023, CIRCLE polled youth (ages 18-34) ahead of the 2024 presidential election. Alberto Medina, the communications team lead at CIRCLE found that the top issues young adults find particularly concerning are the cost of living, inflation, gun violence prevention, addressing climate change, and expanding access to abortion.

These factors will most likely determine who young people will be voting for in 2024. “Young people are that age group that is least likely to identify or belong to a particular party,” Medina says.

As the Center for Information & Research on Civic Living and Engagement's surveys show, young voters' commitment to participate stands as a testament to their generation's evolving passion for political issues. Whether concerned with the cost of living, climate change, or social justice, their engagement marks a pivotal moment in the nation's democratic narrative. As we move towards November, Gen Z's impact on the electoral landscape becomes greatly encouraged.

According to the data from CIRCLE, campaigns this year aren’t doing a great job reaching out to young voters. The data shows that only about 20% of participants said a campaign had attempted to reach out to young people, whether it be through social media or holding some type of event. ”

“Given how much attention there is on young voters and their power and their importance to elections, you would expect more than just 20 percent of you to have heard from a candidate or a party,” Medina said.

Medina said that when young people hear from campaigns during an election cycle, especially when they hear from campaigns multiple times, that increases their likelihood of voting. If they know about the causes the campaign promotes, it changes the attitude of young people, and that's the only way that voting and the election could change.
